I'm Lea Broudo, a software engineer from New York.

Between 2014 and 2018 I taught myself to draw portraits, since I was interested in being able to capture people's likeness on one page. After portraits, I began to work on larger and more surreal scenes, as well as branch off into other mediums like embroidery and pottery. You can check out some of my work here.

While pursuing my undergrad at Columbia I learned to program, but realized I could continue art within engineering. I specialized in computer graphics and vision, and worked on VR projects at Steven Feiner's Computer Graphics and User Interfaces Lab.

Modal Header

HoloBrawl is a VR board game that allows players to personally interact with their pieces. Pieces squirm when picked up, and have personalities of their own! The objective of the game is to kill all of your AI opponent’s pieces before they kill yours. Players must move their pieces to an opponent’s tile to attack them. Each piece has an attack and defense score, and if the difference between the attacker’s attack score and the defense’s defense score is big enough, with a bit of chance the attacker will kill the defense piece. Given different pairings of these scores, pieces can also be counter-killed or pushed to a new tile.
